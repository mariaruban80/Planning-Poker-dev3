Planning Poker is a collaborative estimation technique used by agile teams to estimate the effort of user stories. Our digital Planning Poker app brings this experience online, allowing distributed teams to estimate together in real-time—no matter where your team members are located.

How Planning Poker Works

Create a New Session

Start by creating a new Planning Poker session. Choose your preferred voting scale (Fibonacci, T-shirt sizes, etc.) and set up your session name. You'll become the host with full control over the session.

  • Multiple voting systems to choose from
  • Simple session creation process
  • Automatic room creation with secure link

Invite Your Team

Once your session is created, share the invitation link with your team members. They'll join the session instantly without needing to create an account. Everyone will be able to see who's in the session.

  • Simple one-click invitation links
  • No account creation needed for participants
  • Real-time participant list updates

Add User Stories

Add your user stories or tasks one by one using the "Add Ticket" feature, or import multiple stories at once using the CSV upload option. This lets you quickly populate your session with items to estimate.

  • Individual story/task creation
  • Bulk import via CSV file
  • Easy navigation between stories

Vote Together

Team members select their estimates by choosing a card from the available options. Everyone votes independently and the votes remain hidden until revealed. You can see who has voted without seeing their actual votes.

  • Anonymous voting prevents anchoring bias
  • Visual indicators show who has voted
  • Multiple estimation scales available

Reveal and Discuss

Once everyone has voted, the host can reveal all votes simultaneously. The app displays each person's estimate along with helpful statistics like the average and level of agreement. This information helps facilitate discussions about differences in estimates.

  • Simultaneous vote reveal
  • Automatic calculation of statistics
  • Easy identification of estimation outliers

Frequently Asked Questions

What is Planning Poker and why should we use it?

Planning Poker is a consensus-based estimation technique used by agile teams. Team members make estimates individually, reducing influence bias. It's effective because it:

  • Brings multiple perspectives into the estimation process
  • Reduces anchoring bias by revealing all votes simultaneously
  • Encourages discussion when estimates differ
  • Creates team ownership of the estimates
  • Makes the estimation process more engaging and interactive
How many people can join a Planning Poker session?

The Planning Poker app supports teams of all sizes. We've optimized for 3-12 participants, which is typical for most agile teams, but larger groups are supported as well. The interface automatically adapts to accommodate the number of participants in your session.

Is my data stored on your servers?

Session data is temporarily stored only for the duration of the active session. We don't permanently store your stories or votes. For added privacy, you can export your results at the end of the session if you need to keep a record.

What's the difference between the various voting scales?

Different teams prefer different scales based on their workflow:

  • Fibonacci (0, 1, 2, 3, 5, 8, 13, 21...): Reflects the uncertainty in larger estimates with bigger gaps between higher numbers.
  • Short Fibonacci (0, ½, 1, 2, 3): Better for teams working with smaller, more granular stories.
  • T-Shirt Sizes (XXS to XXL): Intuitive for teams that prefer non-numeric, relative sizing.
  • T-Shirt & Numbers: Combines the intuitiveness of t-shirt sizes with numeric values for easier tracking.
How can I import my backlog from Jira or other tools?

Export your stories from Jira or other tools as a CSV file, then use the "Upload Ticket" feature to import them. The CSV format should have one story per line, with details separated by commas.

What happens if someone gets disconnected during a session?

The app automatically attempts to reconnect users who lose their connection. When they rejoin, they'll see the current state of the session and their previous votes will be preserved.
